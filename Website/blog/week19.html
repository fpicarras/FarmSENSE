Progress Update

16th Jun. - 22nd Jun.

During the past week, we completed the final adjustments to our website. We also started and finished the harvest date prediction algorithm. This algorithm is based on accumulated light and a computer vision model. Using accumulated light, we performed a linear regression, and with the computer vision model, we adjusted the accumulated light threshold value to achieve the desired maturation level (90% to 95%). The computer vision model proved to be quite important, as it allows us to correct potential deviations since maturation is not completely linear with accumulated light. Finally, this model was integrated into the web app.

(Francisco Apolinário)

We also finished the modifications to the 3D models of the boxes that will house the hardware, making them more compact and efficient.

(Hugo Dezerto)

During the week, we also made adjustments to the server and the backend of the web app to integrate the harvest date prediction model. Concurrently, we printed the designed boxes and added stands to ensure the nodes are positioned above the level of the tomato plant leaves. This setup allows for accurate light measurements.

(Filipe Piçarra)

Lastly, we finalized the computer vision model for identifying diseases on tomato leaves.

(Nuno Abreu)
