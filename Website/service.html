Our Project

Problem Defenition

The persistent challenge of controlling the environment and growth levels of agricultural products spans diverse landscapes, from urban rooftops to rural fields. Existing monitoring methods often fall short, failing to adapt to the diverse needs of different crops and leaving small to medium-scale producers underserved. This results in delayed problem identification and less productive harvests, impacting agricultural systems in both urban and rural settings.

Solution Proposal

A scalable solution is imperative to address the growing demand for effective agricultural monitoring, particularly in urban settings witnessing a surge in micro-productions. We envision implementing a grid of wireless sensors, equipped with unique sets of sensors and presets to measure environmental conditions, offering a promising approach. These sensors, designed to be waterproof and solar-powered, would be strategically placed in the soil and connected to a central processing node. This centralized controller facilitates data aggregation and analysis, empowering us to make informed decisions regarding irrigation, pest control, and other critical aspects of crop management.

Solution Conception

The creation of our solution will involve developing and implementing a robust and flexible infrastructure. We will need to design the sensors with the capability to measure a variety of environmental parameters, such as soil moisture, temperature, and sunlight levels. Additionally, integrating reliable and low-power wireless communication technologies will be crucial to ensure efficient data transmission to the central node. Our team will also develop data analysis software that will play a crucial role, allowing for the interpretation of collected data and the generation of actionable insights to optimize crop management.

Areas of Labor

The project encompasses various distinct tasks that need to be executed, each of which contributes to achieving the final result when combined.

Hardware

Responsible for device design, assembly, hardware acquisition, sensor testing, configurations, and energy management.

Software

Setting up and configuring microcontrollers, establishing wireless communication networks, managing crop databases, and creating the webapp.

Computer Vision

Software subdivision integrating a computer vision algorithm for recognizing crop growth stages and relevant data.

Solar Panels & Batteries

Subdivision of hardware but focused on assuring the devices have an efficient power source during the day and night.​

Comunication

Responsible for creating periodic reports, liaising with external partners, coordinating field tests, and designing website, videos, and posters.

  • Webapp

    We built a web app with a Python and Flask backend and a JavaScript frontend featuring secure login, dynamic charts, and data export. It includes weather forecasts via API and a computer vision model for crop monitoring. Technologies used are HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, Flask, and Bootstrap.

    04th June 2024

  • Batteries

    To ensure energy autonomy for the nodes, power consumption and battery options were analyzed. The primary node was grid-powered due to high consumption. For secondary nodes, precise current measurements indicated the average current needed for 4-5 months. 2200 mAh Ni-MH batteries were chosen for their capacity, lifecycle, energy density, and low environmental impact, using three in series per node to match the circuit voltage.

    22nd May 2024

  • Hardware Prototyping

    We developed a compact system for plantation data collection using ground-placed sensors that transmit data via LoRa to a central system. This system acts as a router, sending data to a database for processing and transmission to a farmer's application. Energy optimizations and waterproof enclosures enable efficient, real-time data collection with minimal maintenance until harvest.

    15th May 2024

  • Internodal Communication

    After testing BLE and other options, LoRa was chosen for its low energy use, bandwidth, and long range. We picked raspi-lora for the Raspberry Pi 5 central node and u-lora for the XIAO ESP32S3 Sense auxiliary nodes. The 868 MHz RFM95 LoRa transceiver was used, standard in Europe.

    21st April 2024

  • Mid-Program Pitch Deck

    A mid-term evaluation assessed the project's current status, covering progress, challenges, deviations from the original plan, and other relevant aspects.

    01st April 2024

  • Database & Server

    The project's second phase developed a Python system using Flask to monitor agricultural conditions via sensors. It features a server storing data in SQLite, hosted on a machine with open data access. Basic authentication allows user registration and data access. Simulated sensor data scripts were created for system testing and validation prior to hardware integration.

    26th March 2024
